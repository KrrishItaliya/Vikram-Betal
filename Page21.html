Story:21 The Story of King Shalivahana

In a distant kingdom, there reigned a wise and just king named Shalivahana. He was beloved by his subjects for his fairness and his commitment to the well-being of his kingdom. King Shalivahana was not only known for his noble rule but also for his unwavering adherence to the principles of truth and honesty.

One day, as the king was walking through his royal garden, he discovered a hidden treasure buried beneath the earth. The treasure was vast, glittering with gold, jewels, and precious artifacts. King Shalivahana, being a man of great integrity, knew that the treasure did not belong to him personally but rather to the kingdom and its people.

He summoned his ministers and informed them of the discovery. Together, they decided that the treasure should be used for the betterment of the kingdom. The riches would be used to construct schools, hospitals, and other facilities that would benefit the people, particularly the less fortunate.

The news of the king's decision spread throughout the kingdom, and the people hailed him as a just and compassionate ruler. King Shalivahana's commitment to the welfare of his subjects earned him even greater respect and admiration.

The cunning minister approached the king and pretended to be concerned about the kingdom's financial stability. He suggested that instead of using the treasure for the people's benefit, they should use it to strengthen the royal treasury and enhance the king's wealth and power. He argued that this would make the kingdom even more prosperous in the long run.

King Shalivahana, though wise and just, was not immune to manipulation. He was persuaded by the deceitful minister's arguments and decided to follow his advice. The treasure was moved to the royal treasury, and the minister's plan seemed to be succeeding.

However, as time passed, the consequences of the king's decision became apparent. The people of the kingdom grew disillusioned and began to resent their once-beloved ruler. They saw the promised schools and hospitals never materializing, and their lives did not improve as they had hoped.

Realizing the grave mistake he had made, King Shalivahana summoned his ministers once more and confessed his error. He acknowledged that he had been deceived by the deceitful minister and had betrayed the trust of his people.
