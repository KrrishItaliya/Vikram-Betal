Story:22 The Story of the Cunning Barber

Once upon a time, in a bustling kingdom, there lived a clever and resourceful barber named Gopal. Gopal was not only known for his impeccable grooming skills but also for his sharp wit and cunning intellect. His reputation as the cleverest man in the kingdom was well-deserved, for he often found himself in situations that required quick thinking and clever solutions.

One day, a group of envious men in the kingdom decided to challenge Gopal's intelligence. They came up with a plan to trap him in a web of deceit. These men approached the king and concocted a false story about Gopal plotting to overthrow the kingdom. The king, although initially skeptical, was swayed by their convincing tale and ordered for Gopal to be arrested.

When Gopal was brought before the king, he calmly listened to the accusations and realized that he was facing a grave injustice. However, he knew that protesting his innocence directly would not suffice, for the king had already been misled by the envious men. Instead, Gopal decided to employ his cunning intellect to prove his innocence.

The story of King Shalivahana illustrates the importance of honesty, integrity, and the responsibility of those in power toward their subjects. It also serves as a reminder that even the most just and wise individuals can be misled by deception, but true greatness lies in recognizing and rectifying one's mistakes for the benefit of all. King Shalivahana's commitment to truth and the welfare of his people ultimately defined his legacy as a noble and righteous ruler.

With a confident demeanor, Gopal addressed the king, "Your Majesty, I have been falsely accused, but I have a way to prove my innocence. I can solve a riddle that these accusers cannot answer, and my answer will reveal the truth."

Intrigued by Gopal's proposal, the king agreed. Gopal posed the riddle, "I will give each of these accusers one gold coin, and you, Your Majesty, will provide one as well. Then, we will all go to the royal stable, where I will place my coin in one of the horses' mouths. If, by sunrise tomorrow, the coin is still in the horse's mouth, it will prove my innocence."

The envious men readily accepted the challenge, thinking it impossible for Gopal to escape the trap. The following morning, they anxiously gathered at the royal stable, certain that Gopal's coin would have vanished, thus incriminating him.

However, when the first rays of sunlight touched the stable, Gopal triumphantly retrieved the coin from the horse's mouth. The envious men were left baffled and defeated, unable to explain how Gopal had managed to outsmart them.

With a smile, Gopal revealed the truth to the king and the court. He explained that he had fed the horse a specially prepared meal the previous night that made it incapable of chewing or swallowing the coin. The envious men, unaware of this cunning trick, were left humiliated, and Gopal's innocence was irrefutably established.

The king, recognizing Gopal's intelligence and integrity, not only pardoned him but also rewarded him for his cleverness. The envious men, on the other hand, were held accountable for their false accusations and deceitful scheme.

The story of the cunning barber Gopal teaches us the importance of wit, intelligence, and the ability to think on one's feet in the face of adversity. Gopal's clever solution not only saved him from unjust punishment but also exposed the deceit of his accusers. It serves as a reminder that intelligence and resourcefulness can triumph over false accusations and envy.
