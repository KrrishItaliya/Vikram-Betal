Story:17 The King and the Astrologer

Once upon a time, in a prosperous kingdom, there ruled a wise and just king named Vikramaditya. King Vikramaditya was known far and wide for his intelligence and his ability to solve even the most complex problems. His reputation attracted not only his subjects but also people from neighboring lands, who sought his wisdom and guidance.

One day, a renowned astrologer named Varahamihira arrived at the royal court. He was known for his accuracy in predicting the future, and his fame had reached the ears of King Vikramaditya. The king welcomed the astrologer with great respect and asked him to predict the future.

The astrologer Varahamihira studied the positions of the stars and planets meticulously and then delivered his prophecy. His words were grave, for he foretold that King Vikramaditya would meet an untimely death within a week. The courtiers and ministers were shocked and saddened by this prediction, and King Vikramaditya himself was deeply troubled.

The king, however, was a man of great courage and resolve. He sought advice from his councilors and ministers, who suggested that he leave the kingdom for a week and travel incognito to avoid the predicted fate. King Vikramaditya agreed to this plan and disguised himself as a commoner. He left the palace, choosing to wander through the streets and markets of his own kingdom.

As the week passed, King Vikramaditya explored his kingdom while disguised as a common man. He observed the lives of his subjects, their joys, and their sorrows. He also realized the various challenges and problems faced by his people. Despite the impending doom predicted by the astrologer, the king did not reveal his true identity and continued his journey with a heavy heart.

On the seventh day, King Vikramaditya decided to rest under a banyan tree in a small village. As he sat there, a group of children gathered around him, unaware of his true identity. One of the children was crying inconsolably. King Vikramaditya, moved by the child's distress, approached and asked the reason for the tears.

The child replied, "My father is very ill, and we cannot afford the medicine he needs to get better. I fear we will lose him." The king, touched by the child's plight, immediately gave the family a bag of gold coins, enough to purchase the medicine and more. The child's tears turned into tears of gratitude as he thanked the kind stranger.

As King Vikramaditya continued his journey, he felt a sense of fulfillment and satisfaction in helping his people in their time of need. The day passed, and the predicted week came to an end. King Vikramaditya returned to his palace, prepared to face the prophecy of the astrologer with grace and courage.

When Varahamihira, the astrologer, arrived at the palace to check if his prophecy had come true, he was surprised to see King Vikramaditya alive and well. The king revealed himself and explained his journey, the people he had helped, and how he had realized the importance of compassion and empathy for his subjects.

Varahamihira, the wise astrologer, understood that his prediction had not taken into account the king's good deeds during the week. He praised King Vikramaditya for his humility, compassion, and selflessness, and acknowledged that these virtues had altered the course of destiny.

The story of the king and the astrologer teaches us that good deeds and selflessness have the power to change our fate and destiny. It also emphasizes the importance of empathy, compassion, and understanding the needs of others, even in the face of adversity. King Vikramaditya's actions exemplify the true qualities of a just and compassionate ruler, earning him even greater respect and admiration from his subjects.
