Story:18 The King and the Astrologer

In a prosperous kingdom, there lived a kind and just king named Raja Bhoj. He was known throughout his realm for his wisdom, fairness, and compassion. One of the most cherished places in his kingdom was the royal garden, which was maintained by a loyal and diligent gardener named Haripal.

Haripal had served the king for many years, tending to the beautiful garden with great care and dedication. The garden was a sanctuary of lush greenery, vibrant flowers, and exotic plants. It was a place of tranquility and joy for the people of the kingdom, and they cherished its beauty.

One day, while the king was taking a leisurely stroll in the garden, he overheard a conversation between Haripal and his fellow gardener, Ramu. Ramu was complaining about his low wages and expressing his desire to leave the royal service for better-paying opportunities elsewhere. Haripal, however, spoke passionately about his love for the garden and his unwavering loyalty to the king. He believed that serving the king was not just a job but a noble duty.

Impressed by Haripal's dedication and loyalty, King Raja Bhoj decided to reward him. He called Haripal to his palace and offered him a substantial increase in wages, along with the opportunity to serve in a higher position in the royal court. Haripal, though grateful for the king's generosity, humbly declined the offer, stating that he found his true happiness in caring for the royal garden.

King Raja Bhoj was deeply moved by Haripal's loyalty and selflessness. He realized that such unwavering dedication to duty and genuine love for one's work was a rare and precious quality. The king, instead of insisting on the promotion, decided to honor Haripal in a different way. He ordered that a plaque be installed in the garden with a heartfelt inscription, praising Haripal's loyalty and service. The plaque read, "Haripal, the Loyal Gardener, whose dedication and love for the garden and the kingdom are an inspiration to us all."

Haripal's loyalty and humility touched the hearts of everyone in the kingdom, and he continued to serve as the royal gardener with the same devotion as before. The garden thrived under his care, becoming even more beautiful and enchanting.

The story of the loyal gardener teaches us the value of loyalty, dedication, and the joy that can be found in one's work. Haripal's unwavering commitment to his duty and his selflessness in serving the king and the kingdom serve as a timeless example of the noble qualities that should be celebrated and admired. King Raja Bhoj's recognition of Haripal's loyalty not only honored the gardener but also reinforced the importance of appreciating and valuing those who contribute selflessly to society.
